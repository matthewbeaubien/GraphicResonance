Attraction
—

[psychomotor effect]

Attraction refers to a sound event that halts and draws one's attention consciously or unconsciously. The strength of this attraction can range from fleeting glimpse to one's complete undivided attention. Augoyard and Torgue exemplify buskers or musical performers in a busy street, which contrast the common noise of the urban drone. This effect reminds me of sitting in a quiet classroom and everyone’s attention is diverted for a moment by a tardy student opening the door.

This composition describes the noise surrounding a specific sound that stand out. The red layer of the composition focalizes our attention visually in the composition much like a musical performer does sonically in a subway.
