Ubiquity
—

[mnemo-perceptive effect]

Ubiquity is a spatiotemporal condition which describes a difficulty to determine the point of propagation of a specific sound. Augoyard and Torgue describe that sound and ubiquity are fundamentally linked, as sound is characteristically immaterial. An urban drone or a the sound of a sea does not produce this affect, despite the fact that they are literally omnipresent without a specific origin, (if one is immersed in the context). This physical omnipresence however, is exactly why they do not produce the effect, Augoyard and Torgue argue. The effect manifests when there is a sound which we cannot locate, but which we know has a specific origin. Augoyard and Torgue state, “the uncertainty produced by a sound about its origin established a power relationship between an invisible emitter and the worried receptor.”
