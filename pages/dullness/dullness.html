Dullness
—

[elementary effect]

Opposite to echo, resonance, and reverberation, dullness refers to the deadening of sounds on anechoic and non-reverberatory surfaces. Augoyard and Torgue state that, “The absolute state of dullness is only realized in an anechoic chamber. Searching for complete silence, composer John Cage, could still his own circulatory and nervous systems.”
