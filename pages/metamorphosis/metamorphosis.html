Metamorphosis
—

[mnemo-perceptive effect]

Metamorphosis, derived from the greek “Metablolos,” has roots in the philosophy of the from the pre-Socratic philosopher Heraclitus, who believed that the world is in perpetual change. "You cant step in the same river twice." Likewise, not one sonic context is ever the same. Sound ambiences are constituted by countless layers of different textures. Sound ambiences are subject to a constant state of transition. The relationships between different sonic elements are constantly fluctuating. Furthermore, our subjective perception of these relationships is also perpetually in a state of metamorphosis.

This composition represents metamorphosis in liquid forms which are constantly undulating and contorting with the variable frequencies. Liquids, as the river metaphor suggests, are highly subject to change, just as the sonic landscape is.
