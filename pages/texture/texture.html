Texture
—

[elementary effect]

The texture of a soundscape is determined by a range of sonic qualities including the timbre of individual sounds—which is constituted by the envelope, pitch, intensity, quality and stress—and their interactions with other sounds within the ambiance. Texture is very similar to the colour of a sound, however, is described through a different synesthetic perspective.

This composition uses overlayed layers that interact with one another based on the amplitudes of selected frequency bands across the spectrum. These novel interactions determine the character and texture of the soundscape from the perspective of your microphone.
