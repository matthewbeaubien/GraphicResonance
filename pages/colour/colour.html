Colour

The colour of a sound or soundscape is essentially synonymous with it's texture, albeit from a different synaesthetic perspective. The colour of a sound is comprised by its range of frequencies and the amplitudes thereof. Colours of noise are described in relation to light. For instance, "white noise" is comprised of equal amplitudes of all audible frequencies across the spectrum, much like white light is composed of an equal distribution of colours across the spectrum.
