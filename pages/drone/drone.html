Drone
—

[compositional effect]

The drone effect refers to a constant unchanging layer of sound within a composition. Some instruments, such as bagpipes or a hurry gurdy automatically play drone notes when they are being played. Drone can also be observed in urban or industrial soundscapes through the distortion of distant sounds produced by cars and machinery to form a brownian noise. Similarly can be heard in the blurring of sounds in a natural environment. Drones appear through an electrostatic hum from household appliances.
