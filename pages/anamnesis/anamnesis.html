Anamnesis
—

[mnemo-perceptive effect]

Sonic effect that provokes reminiscence of a memory based on a sonic signal or ambiance. Also a psychological effect where an association is built between a sound and a context. This effect, naturally is very subjective to each passive or active listener. The same sound that evokes a strong memory for one listener may evoke a different memory, or no memory at all for another. This effect distorts perception of time, bringing the past into the present moment. This effect is furthermore, not necessarily voluntary; memories may be revived both actively and passively. Musical composition uses the anamnesis effect by repeating a chorus or theme to bring one back to a certain emotional context. The effect, Augoyard and Torgue tell us, is used frequently in film scores as a device to associate an emotional context to a character or setting and quickly revive that emotional context in the viewer. Eg. One might recall a friend’s face when the specific timbre of their voice is heard. A memory of a childhood might be recalled by the theme song of a T.V. show.
